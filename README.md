# Project_02_Student_Performance_Analysis
Analyzed student performance data to identify key factors influencing academic achievement and provide actionable recommendations.


## ABOUT THE PROJECT
In this project, I analyzed a student performance dataset to understand the factors that affect student scores. The analysis was done using Python in Google Colab. Different factors such as parental education, test preparation, and gender were studied to see how they influence academic performance.

## OBJECTIVES
- Understand student performance data.
- Find factors that affect student scores.
- Analyze the impact of parental education.
- Compare students who completed test preparation with those who did not.
- Study the relationship between math, reading, and writing scores.
- Identify students who may need additional academic support.

## TOOLS USED
- Python
- Pandas
- NumPy
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
- Google Colab

## DATA SET INFORMATION
The dataset contains information about students and their scores in:

- Mathematics
- Reading
- Writing

It also includes details such as:

- Gender
- Race/Ethnicity
- Parental Level of Education
- Lunch Type
- Test Preparation Course

## ANALASIS PERFORMED
### 1. Parental Education Analysis
Analyzed whether parental education level has an effect on student performance.

### 2. Test Preparation Analysis
Compared the scores of students who completed the test preparation course and those who did not.

### 3. Correlation Analysis
Studied the relationship between mathematics, reading, and writing scores.

### 4. Gender-Based Analysis
Compared the average performance of male and female students.

### 5. Score Distribution Analysis
Analyzed how student scores are distributed across the dataset.

### 6. At-Risk Student Analysis
Identified students who scored below the expected level and may require extra academic support.

## KEY FINDINGS
- Students who completed the test preparation course generally scored higher.
- Reading and writing scores showed a strong positive relationship.
- Students whose parents had higher education levels tended to perform better.
- Female students performed better in reading and writing, while male students performed slightly better in mathematics.
- Some students were identified as academically at-risk.

## CONCLUSION
This project helped in understanding the factors that influence student performance. The analysis also identified at-risk students and provided useful recommendations that can help improve academic outcomes.
